From Copyright to Copywriting

When I was about ten years old, everyone I knew kept asking me what I wanted to do when I grew up. I never really knew what to say, but I wanted to know. Even as a child, I was a planner, so I set about coming up with an answer. I found some construction paper in the basement, grabbed one of the approximately 837 crayons that I had and I wrote a list of all the ideas I had.

  • Doctor
  • Author
  • Teacher
  • Playground Designer
  • Astronaut
  • Softball Player
  • Gymnast
  • Artist
  • Businesswoman
  • Dog Groomer
  • Restaurant Manager
  • Baker

When my list was finished, I neatly cut them into strips and folded them in half so I couldn't see what was on them. Then I threw them in the air and let them all fall on the floor of my bedroom. I closed my eyes and knelt by the mess of paper strips and I told myself that whatever was on the slip of paper that I picked up was going to be what I was going to be when I grew up. I reached out, picked up a piece of paper, unfolded it, and saw my destiny before me.

Author

I took that slip of paper all the way to a Bachelors degree in English in 2011. Since then, I've been a wordsmith, writing everything from novels to feature length movie scripts, travel itineraries to listicles. I became a master of MLA, APA, AP, and Chicago styles. I can adapt to and write in any voice that a brand might need, all while keeping things creative, unique, and professional.
